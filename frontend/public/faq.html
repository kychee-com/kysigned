FAQ

Short answers for people signing by email and teams running their own Kysigned instance.

How do I sign?

Open the signing email, read the attached PDF, then forward the whole email back to the signing address. Put the required signing phrase as the first line of your forward and keep the PDF attached.

What if I forward from the wrong email address?

Kysigned checks the email address that created the DKIM signature. Forward from the same address that received the signing request, or ask the document creator to send a new request to the address you want to use.

Do signers need an account?

No. Signers use their existing email provider. The creator needs access to the Kysigned dashboard, but signers can complete a request by forwarding email.

Why do document creators need to sign in?

The creator account is where sent envelopes live: it ties each envelope to its owner, shows signing progress, and delivers the completed evidence bundle. You can draft an envelope without an account; sign-in happens once, at “Send for signing”, via a magic link to your email. There is no password and no separate registration: your account is created on your first sign-in.

Signers never sign in. They forward the signing email back with the signing phrase, and that is it.

Can I verify a completed bundle later?

Yes. The evidence bundle carries the document, signer emails, DKIM evidence, timestamps, and verification metadata. Use the verifier at /verify or the repository CLI.

Which languages can I use for names?

Document titles and signer names printed on the cover and signature pages support Latin (with accents), Greek, Cyrillic, Hebrew, and Arabic. Hebrew is laid out right to left; Arabic renders in standalone letter shapes rather than joined cursive. Chinese, Japanese, and Korean names are not supported yet: a name using a character the cover font cannot render is rejected at creation, with the exact character named, so nothing is ever printed as an empty box. This applies only to the names Kysigned prints. The uploaded document is never re-typeset, so its own contents can be in any language.

Can I run my own copy?

Yes. Clone the open-source repository, set KYSIGNED_ALLOWED_CREATORS, then run run402 up --name your-name. Run402 provisions the project, mailboxes, secrets, site, database, and function routes declared in run402.json.

Kysigned is not a substitute for legal advice. Check whether electronic signatures are valid for your use case and jurisdiction.
